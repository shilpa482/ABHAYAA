Thyroid in Women

Thyroid has become a significant health problem in women. Hormone imbalances also occur with weight gain in the thyroid. According to one study, women have ten times more thyroid than men. The main reason for this is the high prevalence of autoimmune problems in women. According to an SRL Diagnostics report, 20 per cent of women were found positive for thyroid antibodies, compared to 15 per cent of men. 18 per cent of the population aged 31 to 45 have been found positive for thyroid antibodies. The largest number of people in northern India is positive for thyroid antibodies. The thyroid disorder affects the thyroid gland, a butterfly-shaped organ located in the front of the neck below Adams apple. Hormones produced and stored by the thyroid gland affect the functioning of various body parts. These hormones regulate the bodys metabolic rate, heart and digestive systems functioning, brain development, control of muscles, bones, and mood maintenance.

Breast Cancer in Women

In most cases, breast cancer cells form a tumour that can often be felt as a lump or seen on an X-ray. Breast cancer is not only growing at a rapid pace but has also overtaken all other cancers to emerge as the most common cancer among women. It occurs almost in women, but men can also get breast cancer in rare cases. It is necessary to be aware of the fact that most breast lumps are not cancerous. Non-cancerous breast tumours are just abnormal growths that do not spread outside the breast. Although non-cancerous tumours are not life-threatening, some types of women may have an increased risk of developing breast cancer. If a breast lump is felt, it is imperative to have it checked by a certified physician to determine whether it is benign or malignant and whether it may affect your future cancer risk. Besides, awareness and regular screening of symptoms are essential ways to reduce risk. With rapidly increasing disease incidence, particularly among women under 50 years of age, women need to step up surveillance and screenings.

Osteoporosis in Women

Women with perimenopause have a 56% increased risk of osteoporosis in their 70s. Early menopause is a springboard for many health conditions in women, and osteoporosis is one of them. Mostly all women experience a drop in bone density when they go through menopause. But women in early menopause have more chances of weak and brittle bones than their female counterparts, who have a late menopause.

Cervical Cancer in Women

Cervical cancer is the most common cause of death of women worldwide. Do you know that this cancer is becoming common in Indian women as well? Yes, if you look at the statistics, this cancer is becoming the second largest cause of death among women due to lack of timely treatment. But it is a cancerous disease that can be avoided or cured at the right time. Every year, there are approximately 1,22,000 new cancer cases in India, of which around 67,500 are women. Cervical cancer is the cause of 11.1 per cent of all cancer-related deaths. This situation gets worse because only 3.1 per cent of the women in the country are screened for this condition, due to which the remaining women live under the shadow of danger.

Health tips for women:

Let us know how a woman can keep her heart, mind and body healthy

Exercise every day

The more active you are, the better it is for your heart health. Regular exercise makes our heart healthy, increases muscles and bones strength, and still remove health problems. Do 2 to 4 hours of moderate activity a week, such as brisk walking or dancing. Apart from this, you can do activities like running 1 hour 15 minutes a week or playing tennis. Also, focus on increasing your power and strength. If you are busy with office work, try doing small activities throughout the day. Walk as much as you can, aiming to walk at least 10,000 steps a day. Use the stairs instead of the lift. Park your car away from your destination.

Follow a heart-healthy diet

If you want to keep away from heart stroke, diseases and other heart problems, you must follow this diet. Choose whole grains, try a brown rice instead of white. Instead of refined flour, switch to pasta made from wheat. Cut down on heavily processed food, sugar, salt, and saturated fat. Eat more and more fruits and vegetables. If you are following a healthy diet, flexibility in the diet is often useful. If you prefer to follow a strict diet plan, then the above diet plan is the best, if not, eat what is helpful for you.

Keep your weight in control

If you keep weight under control, you are at a reduced risk of heart disease, type 2 diabetes and cancer. For this, you lose weight slowly (1-2 pounds a week) - be active and have a good diet. Exercise is essential to losing weight. Do you want to lose weight? Then try exercising 300 minutes a week. Reduce sugar from your diet, also try to avoid coffee drinks containing soda and sugar.

Visit the doctor from time to time

Check your body regularly. Your doctor can help you stay healthy with the help of your medical history. Your doctor may recommend screening tests to check your health and the right treatment options. Talk to your doctor on issues related to your health. "If you have any questions, ask your doctor". If you are concerned about medicine or procedure, talk to him about it.

Reduce stress

If you take stress or stress, it can affect your health. You cannot avoid it altogether, but you can control it by knowing how to reduce its effects. If you want to relieve stress, try these: breathing exercises, yoga, massage, work out, nutritious food.
